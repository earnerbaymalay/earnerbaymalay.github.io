## 🌌 Welcome to Aether
Aether is not just another chat interface; it is a **Local-First Neural Operating Interface**. Unlike cloud-based LLMs, Aether lives on your hardware, giving you total privacy, zero latency, and a persistent cognitive memory.

---

## 🧭 Exploring the Ecosystem

### 1. The Neural Axis (Navigation)
The sidebar is your command center.
- **Pathways**: Where you choose your "cognitive lens" (the model) for the task at hand.
- **Terminal**: The core execution engine. This is where you interact with the neural agent.
- **Vault**: Your persistent knowledge base. Aether saves "fragments" of your conversations here to remember facts across sessions.
- **Models**: The weight manager. Download and organize different LLMs locally.
- **Nexus**: The system optimizer. Hardens your OS privacy and boosts hardware performance.

### 2. The Synapse (Execution)
The central area is where the work happens. When you select a **Pathway**, you aren't just picking a model; you are initializing a specialized agent tailored for that specific type of reasoning (Code, Logic, Turbo, or General Agent).

### 3. The Peripheral (Status)
The right panel provides a real-time HUD of your neural links, hardware health, and quick access to core system tools.

---

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Tier 1: The Novice (First Contact)
1. **Pick a Pathway**: Click on a card (e.g., `AGENT`) to start.
2. **Chat**: Type your request in the terminal.
3. **Switch**: Use the "Switch Pathway" button to change models without losing your progress.

### Tier 2: The Intermediate (Expanding the Mind)
- **AetherVault**: Open the Vault to see what Aether has "distilled" from your conversations. You can manually edit these memories to steer the agent's future behavior.
- **Model Management**: Use the Model Manager to pull the latest weights (e.g., `deepseek-r1`) directly from the local provider.

### Tier 3: The Architect (System Mastery)
- **Nexus Shield**: Enable "AI Bloat Killer" and "Ghost Mode" to reclaim system resources from background OS telemetry.
- **MCP Engines**: (Advanced) Configure Model Context Protocol servers in Settings to give Aether access to your local filesystem, databases, or external APIs securely.
